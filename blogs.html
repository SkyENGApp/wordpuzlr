Choosing Your Difficulty

The complexity of a puzzle changes drastically based on the length of the target word:

  • 3–4 Letters: Perfect for high-speed "blitz" sessions. These focus on common suffixes and vowel placement.
  • 5 Letters: The gold standard. Requires a balance of elimination and vowel hunting.
  • 6–7 Letters: Advanced logic territory. Here, understanding compound words and prefixes becomes your greatest asset.

Core Solving Strategies

To maintain a high win streak, you need a systematic approach. Most master players follow these three rules:

  1. The Vowel Hunt: Your first two guesses should ideally cover A, E, I, O, and U.
  2. Consonant Clustering: Look for common pairs like TH, CH, ST, and PL.
  3. Elimination Logic: Focus as much on what the word isn't as what it is.
